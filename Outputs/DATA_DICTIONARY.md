# freenic Data Dictionary

## Overview

- **Total rows**: 4,965,894,682 (~4.97B, SUM over all base tables in main + catalog + dict)
- **Base tables**: 59 (46 main + 6 catalog + 7 dict)
- **Views**: 52 shaped/convenience views
- **Variables cataloged**: 13,147
- **Cross-source crosswalk**: 76 variable mappings across 4 sources
- **Temporal coverage**: 1863-2026 (latest period_end 2026-05-29)

> **Authoritative counts.** The numbers above are the live warehouse totals, regenerated by
> `tools/freenic_live_counts.py` â†’ `Technical/coverage_analysis/live_counts.json` and asserted
> against this doc by `tools/check_doc_counts.py` (wired into the test suite as
> `tests/test_doc_counts.py`). The three billion-row tables are `call_report_filings` (1.92B),
> `ubpr_ratios` (1.25B), and `ncua_5300` (1.18B); the platform now also includes UBPR peer
> stats/rank, FR Y-15, HMDA, the extended-NIC identity layer, the `dict` taxonomy layer, and the
> canonical `clean_bank_panel`. (The earlier "37 tables / 2.26B rows / 10 views" framing predates
> all of these additions.)

> **Start here (front door from inside the data):** the warehouse is self-describing.
> Every table/view has a row in **`main.freenic_manifest`** (`name, kind, purpose, grain,
> units, code_column, canonical_or_derived, use_instead, row_count, period_span`), and every
> covered fact table + key columns carry DuckDB `COMMENT ON` strings (visible in `DESCRIBE`
> and `duckdb_columns()`). To orient yourself before reading this dict:
>
> ```sql
> SELECT name, purpose, units, code_column, use_instead FROM freenic_manifest ORDER BY kind, name;
> ```
>
> **Units:** see **`UNITS.md`** for the full per-table rule and the `/1e9` trap. Short
> version: Call/FDIC/BHCF/UBPR-dollar are **USD thousands**; NCUA, OCC, and
> `clean_bank_panel` are **USD whole dollars**; `robin_panel` absolute-$ columns are
> uncalibrated (use `clean_bank_panel`). The on-DB comments + manifest are regenerated by
> `Technical/freenic_ingestion/scripts/47_self_describing.py`.

## Which table should I use? (canonical-table guide)

The platform has several overlapping tables on purpose (different sources, grains, vintages).
Pick by what you actually want; the manifest's `use_instead` column points the same way.

| You want... | Use | Not | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Dollar LEVELS** for a bank over time (any era) | **`clean_bank_panel`** (`*_nominal`/`*_real`, whole USD) | `robin_panel` absolute-$ cols | `clean_bank_panel` is the canonical from-raw, unit-verified level panel; `robin_panel`'s absolutes are uncalibrated (~5Ã—â€“14Ã— off). |
| Engineered **ratios + failure flags** per bank-year | **`fdic_sdi_features`** | `fdic_financials` | `fdic_sdi_features` is the derived feature panel (roa, nim, F1/F3/F5_failure); `fdic_financials` is the raw long line items. |
| Raw **bank-level** financial line items (1984+) | **`fdic_financials`** (bank, FDIC SDI) | `bhcf_filings` | `fdic_financials` is the BANK; `bhcf_filings` is the HOLDING COMPANY (consolidated). |
| **Holding-company** consolidated financials | **`bhcf_filings`** (FR Y-9C) | `fdic_financials` | Holding-co level vs bank level â€” they differ for multi-bank BHCs. |
| Pre-1976 historical Call data | **`luck_call_reports`** / `luck_wide`, or `clean_bank_panel` (MODL stratum) | `call_report_filings` | Modern Call series starts 1976Q1; Luck reconstructs 1959-1975. |
| Cross-source financials by **concept** (not code) | **`cross_source_financials`** view (via `variable_crosswalk`) | manual per-source joins | Concept names (`total_assets`, `net_income`) resolve across BHCF/Luck/FDIC. |
| Credit-union financials | **`ncua_5300`** (keyed by `cu_number`, whole dollars) | the bank tables | Separate universe; not keyed by `rssd_id`. |

> Detailed per-table notes follow below; this table is the fast index.

### `luck_call_reports` â€” Luck Historical Database (37,753,719 rows)

> **Units:** dollar values are **USD thousands**. Code column: `variable_id`. For humans: `luck_wide` or `clean_bank_panel` (MODL stratum).

only pre-2001 / CDR-unavailable cells remain here. 16,807 entities, 245 variables.
**Provenance:** free public NY Fed dataset, "Balance Sheets and Income Statements of Commercial
Banks: 1959â€“2025" (released Dec 2025; mirror finhist.com); citation-required (Correia, Luck &

### `occ_historical` â€” OCC Balance Sheets 1863-1941 (17,775,763 rows)

> **Units:** dollar values are **USD WHOLE DOLLARS** (NOT thousands; `/1e9` â†’ billions). Keyed by OCC charter `bank_id`. For humans: `clean_bank_panel` (HIST stratum).

National bank balance sheet data from the Office of the Comptroller of the Currency. Original
1867-1904 layer (9,788,940 rows, 7,109 banks, 95 variables, `source='occ_historical'`) plus the Phase 9b
OCC-CLV finhist extension (7,986,823 rows, 14,258 banks, 66 variables, `source='occ_historical_clv'`),
together spanning **1863-11-28 to 1941-12-31**.

> **Consumer note â€” TWO stacked vintages share this one table, distinguished by `source` (added 2026-06-14, Bev Testing CLV reconstruction):**
> - `source='occ_historical'` â€” the original OCC TSV layer, **1867-10-07 to 1904-09-06**, 95 variables.
> - `source='occ_historical_clv'` â€” the finhist `historical-call.dta` extension, **1863-11-28 to 1941-12-31**, 66 variables.
>
> The two vintages use **different, only-partly-overlapping variable namespaces** (`variable_id` values),
> so a query that does not filter or pivot by `source` may silently mix incompatible variable definitions.
> **Both vintages are clean NOMINAL dollars** (unlike `robin_panel`'s uncalibrated levels â€” see that table),
> making `occ_historical` the correct source for absolute historical bank-level dollar magnitudes.
> *(Span/`source`/variable-count figures verified read-only against this warehouse 2026-06-14; note the
> per-row `source` labels are `occ_historical` / `occ_historical_clv`.)*
>
> **TYPED VIEWS (W4 guard, `scripts/48_footgun_guards.py`, 2026-06-17):** to make the two-vintage split
> safe, query one vintage through its typed view instead of filtering by hand:
> - **`occ_1867_1904`** = `occ_historical WHERE source='occ_historical'` (9,788,940 rows).
> - **`occ_clv_1863_1941`** = `occ_historical WHERE source='occ_historical_clv'` (7,986,823 rows).
>
> The two views are **disjoint** and sum to the 17,775,763-row base table. **DO NOT union them blindly**
> (incompatible `variable_id` namespaces). The base table + both views carry `COMMENT ON` warnings.

| Column | Type | Description |

| bank_id | INTEGER | OCC bank identifier |
| report_date | DATE | Report date |
| variable_id | VARCHAR | Variable name (e.g., assets, deposits, securities) |
| value | DOUBLE | Reported value |
| source | VARCHAR | Source label |

## Convenience Views

### `bhcf_enriched`
BHCF filings joined with institution names and MDRM variable descriptions. Use this instead of manual joins for enriched analysis.

### `current_hierarchy`
Active parent-child relationships only (dt_end IS NULL). Includes institution names for both parent and subsidiary.

### `entity_summary`
Per-entity summary with filing counts, date ranges, and coverage metrics.

### `variable_dictionary`
Variables with full MDRM descriptions and coverage statistics. Use for data discovery.

### `bank_failures_enriched` (4,119 rows)
Bank failures joined with institution details (RSSD ID, entity type, primary federal regulator).

### `deposit_market_share` (822,652 rows)
Per-institution annual deposit market share by state and county, computed from FDIC Summary of Deposits.

### `stress_test_summary` (18,581 rows)
DFAST results filtered to Severely Adverse scenario only â€” the binding constraint for regulatory capital planning.

### `cross_source_financials` (118,462,126 rows)
Unified financial data across BHCF, Luck, and FDIC SDI via the variable crosswalk. Enables concept-level queries (e.g., "total_assets") that automatically span all sources. Columns: rssd_id, institution_name, period_end, concept, source_variable, source_table, value.

### `robin_panel_enriched` (2,867,936 rows)
Robin panel joined with RSSD and FDIC cert identifiers via robin_crosswalk. All 156 Robin columns plus rssd_id, fdic_cert, name_ffiec, match_confidence, ffiec_entity_type, rssd_id_bhc. Use for linking Robin historical data to modern regulatory identifiers.

### `failure_timeline` (17 columns)
Combined failure records from Robin (failed_bank indicator) and FDIC bank_failures, matched via robin_crosswalk. Columns: bank_id, canonical_bank_name, state_abbrev, year, assets, deposits, equity, failed_bank, receivership_date, time_to_fail, bank_run_indicator, rssd_id, fdic_cert, fdic_name, fdic_closing_date, acquiring_institution, fund.
