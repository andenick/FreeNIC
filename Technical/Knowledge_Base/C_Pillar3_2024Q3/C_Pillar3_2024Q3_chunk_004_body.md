# COUNTERPARTY CREDIT RISK: OTC DERIVATIVE CONTRACTS, REPO-STYLE TRANSACTIONS AND ELIGIBLE MARGIN LOANS

## Counterparty Credit Risk Exposures

Counterparty credit risk is the risk that the counterparty to a transaction could default before the final settlement of the transaction's cash flows. For derivatives, counterparty credit risk also arises from unsettled security, commodity and foreign exchange transactions with a contractual settlement or delivery lag that is longer than the lesser of the market standard for the particular instrument or five business days (long settlement transactions).

Repo-style transactions consist of repurchase or reverse repurchase transactions, or securities borrowing or securities lending transactions, including transactions in which Citi acts as agent for a customer and indemnifies the customer against loss, and are based on securities taken or given as collateral, which are marked-to-market, generally daily.

Eligible margin loans are extensions of credit collateralized by liquid and readily marketable debt or equity securities, or gold, and that satisfy other conditions under the U.S. Basel III rules.

### Methodology Used to Assign Economic Capital

Citi calculates economic capital for counterparty credit risk by simulating the potential loss resulting from counterparty defaults. For OTC derivative contracts, the economic capital calculation further captures the potential market rate driven changes in each counterparty's CVA, using a process that integrates stress scenarios and Monte Carlo simulation.

### Methodology Used to Assign Credit Limits

Single name concentration credit limits are managed at the relationship level. Relationship exposure includes the aggregate of all credit products, including loans, counterparty credit risk, trade and transaction services, etc.

### Counterparty Credit Risk Capital Calculations

Citi uses a Monte Carlo simulation to determine an expected positive exposure (EPE) measure as input to EAD calculation. The model utilizes a standard supervisory alpha multiplication factor of 1.4. Citi also uses SA-CCR for certain counterparty credit risk exposures. To calculate EAD for repo-style transactions and eligible margin loans within prime lending portfolios, Citi uses the simple VaR methodology. For positions that do not use simple VaR, Citi uses the (supervisory) collateral haircut approach. Counterparty credit risk treatment also includes an explicit CVA RWA calculation.

### Derivative Master Netting Agreements

Credit risk from derivatives is mitigated where possible through netting agreements. ISDA master agreements are Citi's preferred manner for documenting OTC derivatives.

### Policies for Securing, Valuing and Managing Collateral, and Establishing Credit Reserves

Businesses are required to establish standard eligibility criteria for collateral usage and review processes. Industry standard legal agreements combined with internal reviews for legal enforceability are used to achieve a perfected security interest in the collateral. Daily margining is considered best practice.

### Primary Types of Collateral

Eligible collateral including cash, G10 Government and Government-Sponsored Enterprises debt securities is collected, posted or exchanged as margin on OTC derivative transactions. For repo-style transactions and eligible margin loans, the majority of collateral is cash, government debt securities (mostly investment grade), and public equity securities.

### Policies With Respect to Wrong-Way Risk Exposures

Wrong-way risk (WWR) occurs when a movement in a market factor causes Citi's exposure to a counterparty to increase at the same time as the counterparty's capacity to meet its obligations is decreasing. Citi's WWR policy provides best practices and guidelines for the identification, approval, reporting and mitigation of specific and general WWR. Citi's WWR policy further uses ongoing product stress testing to identify potential general WWR.

### Impact of Citi Credit Rating Downgrade on Collateral Pledged

In the event that Citigroup and Citibank were downgraded a single notch by all three major rating agencies as of September 30, 2024, the Company could be required to post an additional $0.2 billion as either collateral or settlement. Additionally, the Company could be required to segregate $16 million of collateral with third-party custodians, resulting in aggregate cash obligations and collateral requirements of approximately $0.2 billion.

No eligible margin loan made by Citi requires the posting of additional collateral if it were to receive a credit rating downgrade.

### Credit Derivative Notional Amounts

As of September 30, 2024, the net notional amount of outstanding credit protection bought and sold for OTC derivative contracts was approximately $21.6 billion of purchased protection. Citi does not currently use credit derivatives to mitigate counterparty credit risk arising from eligible margin loans.

# CREDIT RISK MITIGATION

## Overview

As part of its risk management activities, Citi uses various risk mitigants to hedge portions of the credit risk in its portfolios, in addition to outright asset sales. Generally, in consultation with legal counsel, Citi determines whether collateral documentation is legally enforceable and gives Citi the right to liquidate or take possession of collateral in the event of default.

### Wholesale Banking Book Collateral: Valuation and Management

Collateral assets taken as credit risk mitigants in the wholesale banking book receive initial and subsequent periodic valuations. Collateral must be realizable and have either (1) a value capable of being established on the open market or (2) a value that can be provided by a recognized external market source or independent valuer.

### Wholesale Banking Book Exposures

The main type of credit risk mitigants utilized for the wholesale banking book exposures are guarantees or other types of full support from parents or third parties, as well as collateral such as real estate or various asset types (securities, receivables, inventories, machinery, etc.).

### Collateral Concentrations

The collateral obtained for Citi's wholesale banking book portfolios is generally well diversified across a wide range of assets. Concentration Risk Identification Thresholds (CRITs) have been set to enable the flagging of outsized collateral holdings.

### Guarantors and Credit Derivative Counterparties

Eligible credit default swap counterparties serving as guarantors of credit risks in the banking book generally include funds, hedge funds, sovereign funds, regional banks, commercial banks, investment banks or insurance companies that are rated BBB- or better by S&P and Moody's.

# SECURITIZATIONS

## Overview

A securitized transaction is a transaction where all or a portion of the credit risk of one or more financial assets is transferred to one or more third parties. Securitizations can either be traditional or synthetic, depending on how the credit risk is transferred.

## Objectives

Citi plays a variety of roles in asset securitization transactions, including originator, sponsor and investor. Citi provides financing through warehouse facilities for corporate loans for CLO issues; consumer assets for ABS issues; and whole mortgage loans for new RMBS and CMBS issues. Citi also provides backstop liquidity facilities to ABCP Conduits and Municipal Tender Option Bond programs.

## Risks

Securitization transactions can involve a number of risks including portfolio risk, seller's risk, and liquidity risk.

## Risk-Based Capital Approaches

Citi utilizes the "hierarchy of approaches" to compute regulatory capital. Citi first calculates using the Supervisory Formula Approach (SFA). Where data is not sufficient, Citi uses the Simplified Supervisory Formula Approach (SSFA). A risk weight of 1,250% is applied to exposures that do not qualify for either approach.

## Securitization Exposures Deducted from Regulatory Capital

As of September 30, 2024 Citi does not have any after-tax gain-on-sale on a securitization that has been deducted from CET1 Capital, nor any credit-enhancing interest-only strips that have been assigned a 1,250% risk weight.

## Re-securitization Exposures Covered by Guarantees

As of September 30, 2024 no re-securitization exposures were covered by guarantees.

# EQUITY EXPOSURES NOT SUBJECT TO THE MARKET RISK CAPITAL RULES

## Overview

Citi holds equity positions to generate capital gains for its private equity subsidiaries, as a result of debt to equity conversions, or to maintain strategic relationships. Citi's equity investments are primarily measured at fair value with changes recognized in net income. However, Federal Reserve Bank and Federal Home Loan Bank stock, as well as certain exchange seats, are presented at cost.

## Risk-Weighting Approaches

Citi applies different approaches:

**Simple Risk Weight Approach** - applicable to equity exposures which are not investment funds; the adjusted carrying value is multiplied by a prescribed risk weight.

**For equity exposures to investment funds**, Citi applies:
- Full Look-Through Approach
- Simple Modified Look-Through Approach
- Alternative Modified Look-Through Approach

## Realized Gains (Losses)

Total net realized gains arising from sales and liquidations of equity investments were $8.1 million for the three months ended September 30, 2024.

## Latent Revaluation Gains (Losses)

Total net unrealized gains on non-marketable equity investments were $16.0 million as of September 30, 2024, none of which was included in Tier 1 or Tier 2 Capital.
