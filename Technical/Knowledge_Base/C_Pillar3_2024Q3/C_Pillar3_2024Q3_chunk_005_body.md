# MARKET RISK

## Overview

Market risk is the risk of loss on a position that could result from movements in market prices. Citi's market risk arises principally from trading and market making activities in Citi's Global Markets businesses, comprising of Fixed Income Markets and Equity Markets.

## Basel III Covered Positions

Covered positions include:

1. Trading assets or trading liabilities (whether on- or off-balance sheet) that meet the following conditions:
   - (a) The position is a "trading position" or hedges another covered position; AND
   - (b) The position is free of any restrictive covenants on its tradability, or Citi is able to hedge the material risk elements.
2. A foreign exchange or commodity position (other than structural foreign currency positions excluded with prior supervisory approval).

Not considered covered positions: (1) intangible assets; (2) hedges outside scope of hedging strategy; (3) ABCP liquidity facilities; (4) positions held with intent to securitize; (5) direct real estate holdings.

Citi has established a firm-wide Basel III Boundary Governance Committee that meets quarterly and serves as a decision-making body on key trading book boundary strategies.

## Valuation and Accounting Policies and Methodologies

Fair value is defined as the price that would be received to sell an asset or paid to transfer a liability in an orderly transaction between market participants at the measurement date (exit price). Material covered positions are carried at fair value on Citi's consolidated balance sheet.

### Determination of Fair Value

Citi uses quoted market prices, when available, to determine fair value. If not available, fair value is based upon internally developed valuation techniques using current market-based parameters. For bonds and secondary market loans traded OTC, Citi uses discounted cash flows, price-based and internal models.

### Market Valuation Adjustments

Valuation adjustments are applied to ensure fair value reflects the price at which a position could be exited. CVA and funding valuation adjustments (FVA) are applied to the relevant population of OTC derivative instruments.

## Market Risk-Weighted Assets

Under the U.S. Basel III rules, Citi's market RWA is the sum of:

- Regulatory Value-at-Risk (VaR)
- Regulatory Stressed Value-at-Risk (SVaR)
- Incremental Risk Charge (IRC)
- Comprehensive Risk Measure (CRM)
- Standard Specific Risk Charge (SSRC)
- Securitization Charges
- De minimis Exposures Charge

## Regulatory Value-at-Risk (VaR)

Regulatory VaR is the estimate of the potential decline in value of a position or portfolio under normal market conditions. Citi uses a three year (18 months for commodities) look back period. Market risk factors include material first and second-order risk sensitivities of various asset classes/risk types (interest rate, credit spread, foreign exchange, equity, and commodity risks).

Citi uses a single, independently approved Monte Carlo simulation VaR model. As of March 31, 2022, the Monte Carlo simulation involves approximately 550,000 market factors, making use of approximately 480,000 time series. Citi uses a 99% confidence interval and a 10-day time horizon for Regulatory VaR.

## Regulatory VaR Backtesting

Citi is required to perform backtesting to evaluate the effectiveness of its VaR model. The Regulatory VaR and SVaR capital multipliers can range between 3 and 4, based on the number of backtesting exceptions on a rolling 12-month period. Based on a 99% confidence level, Citi would expect two to three days in any one year where buy-and-hold losses exceeded the Regulatory VaR. Losses observed have not exceeded Citi's 1-day 99% Regulatory VaR during the three month period ending September 30, 2024.

## Regulatory Stressed Value-at-Risk (SVaR)

Citi's Regulatory SVaR model methodology is the same as the Regulatory VaR methodology (99% confidence level and 10-day holding period), except it uses model parameters calibrated to historical data from a continuous 12-month period that reflects significant financial stress.

## Incremental Risk Charge (IRC)

IRC represents a charge to cover the default and credit migration risks of non-securitized credit products. IRC is measured over a 1-year time horizon at a 99.9% confidence level under the assumption of constant positions. Citi's IRC model captures market and issuer-specific concentrations, credit quality and liquidity horizons.

## Comprehensive Risk Measure (CRM)

CRM is primarily comprised of correlation trading securitization positions within Markets and Securities Services. The CRM is equal to the greater of: (i) a model-based measure and (ii) total specific risk add-ons for modeled correlation trading positions (CRM Floor), calculated as 8% of the greater of the sum of specific risk add-ons for net long or net short correlation trading positions.

Citi received supervisory approval to remove the previously required CRM model surcharge for correlation trading portfolios.

## Standard Specific Risk Charge (SSRC)

Specific risk is the risk of loss from changes in the market value of a position that could result from factors other than broad market movements, including event risk, default risk and idiosyncratic risk. Standard specific risk charges are derived for debt or equity positions which have not received a modeled specific risk charge.

## De minimis Exposures Charge

Applied to covered positions not captured in Citi's Regulatory VaR model. The sum of the absolute value of these positions is multiplied by 12.5 to arrive at applicable RWA.

## Market Risk Management

Citi manages the market risk of covered positions under established standards, policies, and governance frameworks. The measurement used for covered and non-covered trading positions includes: VaR, Stress Testing, Factor Sensitivities, and Internal Model Review and Validation.

### Securitization and Re-securitization Positions

Citi manages these within an established risk management policy framework. Securitization due diligence analysis is completed in accordance with U.S. Basel III rules, including pre-trade analysis and supporting documentation within three days of the trade date.

### Independent Validation of Models

Market risk models are independently validated by Model Risk Management and approved for use.

### Stress Testing

Citi performs stress testing on a regular basis using two complementary approaches: top-down systemic stresses and bottom-up business specific stresses.

# OPERATIONAL RISK

## Overview

Operational risk is the risk of loss resulting from inadequate or failed internal processes, people and systems or from external events. This includes legal risk. Operational risk can result in losses from events associated with:

- fraud, theft and unauthorized activity
- employment practices and workplace environment
- clients, products and business practices
- physical assets and infrastructure
- execution, delivery and process management

## Operational Risk Measurement

Under the U.S. Basel III rules, Citi employs an Advanced Measurement Approaches (AMA) model. Citi utilizes units of measure defined primarily by the type of operational risk event (e.g., fraud or employment practices), but also considering business unit(s) and geography(ies). For each unit of measure, potential loss severity and frequency are modeled independently.

The modeled operational risk losses across units of measure are aggregated, considering correlation across measurement units. Results are modified quarterly by applying a "qualitative adjustment factor" to reflect the current business and control environment.

Citi conducts scenario analysis by major global business. Citi uses insurance for partially mitigating operational risk; however, such insurance does not have a material impact on operational risk capital.

### Independent Review of Models

Operational risk models are independently validated by Model Risk Management and approved for use.
